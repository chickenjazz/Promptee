# Heuristic Scorer SOP

## 1. Goal
To autonomously and deterministically evaluate a given prompt based on three criteria: Clarity, Specificity, and Semantic Preservation.

## 2. Inputs
- `raw_prompt`: The original instruction provided by the user.
- `optimized_prompt`: The rewritten instruction generated by the model (applicable during post-optimization scoring and dataset generation).

## 3. Tool Logic & Processing

### A. Clarity Score (0.0 to 1.0)
- **Engine**: `spaCy` (en_core_web_sm)
- **Logic**: Analyzes syntactic dependency. A clear prompt contains actionable verbs (ROOT verbs) and clear direct objects (`dobj`).
- **Calculation**: Ratio of actionable sentences to total sentences, penalized for excessive noun chunks without verb correlations.

### B. Specificity Score (0.0 to 1.0)
- **Engine**: `spaCy`
- **Logic**: Measures the presence of constraints, modifiers, and specific context.
- **Calculation**: Evaluates the density of adjectives (`amod`), numbers (`nummod`), and named entities (`ENT`) relative to prompt length.

### C. Semantic Preservation Constraint
- **Engine**: `sentence-transformers` (`all-MiniLM-L6-v2`)
- **Logic**: Ensures the `optimized_prompt` does not hallucinate or deviate from the original `raw_prompt`. Emulates BERTScore Recall.
- **Calculation**: Computes the cosine similarity between the contextual embeddings of the raw and optimized prompt.
- **Constraint**: If `cosine_similarity(raw, optimized) < 0.75` (threshold configurable), the rewrite is REJECTED.

### D. Composite Score
- **Formula**: `Score = (W_c * Clarity) + (W_s * Specificity)`
- *Note*: Semantic preservation acts as a gating threshold, or its score is added if successful.

## 4. Edge Cases
- **Empty prompts**: Return 0.0 for all scores.
- **Short prompts**: May suffer in specificity. The system must not over-penalize if the task doesn't require constraints.
